For our dear family and friends travelling to Kota Kinabalu and looking for recommendations, here's a little list of must-visit places, activities, and seafood restaurants to make your trip extra special!

Gaya Street Night Market

A bustling street market offering local food, handmade goods, and viral foods every Friday and Saturday night.

Go Here!

Gaya Street Sunday Market

A bustling street market offering local food, handmade goods, and unique souvenirs every Sunday morning.

Go Here!

Tanjung Aru Beach

A beautiful beach with stunning sunsets, perfect for a relaxing day by the sea, some have even reported it being ranked 4th best sunset in the world by publications like Time Magazine!

Go Here!

Handicraft Market

A bustling marketplace offering local handicrafts, souvenirs, textiles, pearls, and traditional goods, popular with both tourists and locals for authentic keepsakes.

Go Here!

Jesselton Point

A waterfront area where you can catch ferries to nearby islands and enjoy scenic views.

Go Here!

Mari-Mari Cultural Village

A cultural village in the rainforest showcasing traditional houses, food, and dances of Sabah's ethnic tribes.

Go Here!

Island Hopping

Five tropical islands near Kota Kinabalu with white-sand beaches, coral-rich snorkeling spots, and short jungle trails, just a short boat ride from the city.

Go Here!

Fatt Kee

A popular restaurant known for its delicious fish soup, roasted meats and local Chinese dishes.

Go Here!

Gayang Seafood Restaurant

Rustic seaside spot serving fresh live seafood at reasonable prices, praised for its freshness and scenic sea view on the Tuaran hilltop.

Go Here!

Welcome Seafood Asia City

Busy city-center seafood restaurant where diners pick live seafood from tanks, known for variety and convenience.

Go Here!
